Questions to Ask Them
These are specific to Gluon — shows you did your homework:

"Your product covers a lot of modules — finance, inventory, HR, production. Do junior developers work across modules or do they own a specific one early on?"
"I noticed Gluon is also in Dubai — does the team collaborate across offices or are they separate?"
"What does onboarding look like for a junior developer joining the backend team?"


One Thing to Naturally Mention
Since the interview is online and they might not ask about projects — find a way to drop this naturally:

"I actually have two projects that are quite relevant to what Gluon does — POS systems with inventory, sales, and role-based access. If it would be helpful I'm happy to briefly show the code or the running app."

Even if they say no, you've planted the seed that you have something concrete to show.

The overall tone to maintain throughout:
You're not desperate, you're genuinely interested. You researched them specifically, your background lines up with their product, and you're ready to move fast once you're given direction. That's the story — tell it confidently.
