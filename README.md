# Interview Prep Tips

A practical checklist to help you prepare for technical interviews.

## 1. Resume (Start Here)

- Keep your resume clear and one page.
- Make it ATS-friendly:
  - Use simple formatting and standard section headings (Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education).
  - Include keywords from the job description naturally.
  - Avoid graphics, icons, text boxes, and complex tables.
- Focus on impact in bullet points: start with action verbs and include results where possible.
- Proofread carefully for grammar and consistency.

## 2. Projects and Technologies You Can Defend

- Put only those projects and technologies in your CV that you truly understand.
- If you built projects with AI help, learn everything used in them:
  - Architecture and end-to-end pipeline.
  - Each major file/module and why it exists.
  - Libraries, APIs, models, prompts, and deployment flow.
- Be ready to defend design choices, trade-offs, and implementation details in interviews.

## 3. Technical Preparation

- Practice core data structures and algorithms regularly.
- Review common problem patterns (arrays, strings, trees, graphs, dynamic programming).
- Practice writing clean, explainable solutions out loud.

## 4. Behavioral Preparation

- Prepare STAR examples (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for teamwork, conflict, failure, and leadership.
- Keep examples concise and outcome-focused.

## 5. Company and Role Research

- Understand the company product, team, and role expectations.
- Match your experience to what the role needs.

## 6. Mock Interviews

- Do timed mock interviews with peers or online platforms.
- Review weak areas and improve them before the real interview.

## 7. Interview Day Tips

- Wear formal dress and look presentable; first impression is the last impression.
- Test your setup (internet, mic, camera) if remote.
- Join 5-10 minutes early.
- Clarify requirements before coding and communicate your thought process.

## 8. After the Interview

- Send a short thank-you note.
- Write down questions asked and what to improve for next time.
