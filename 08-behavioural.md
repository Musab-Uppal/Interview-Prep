# 🤝 Behavioural Interview Prep

> **Interview Priority:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Technical skills get you in the room; soft skills get you the offer.

---

## 📄 1. Resume

> 💡 Your resume is the first filter. If it doesn't pass an ATS or a 10-second skim, you never get to the interview.

- Keep it **one page** — clear, concise, and relevant.
- Add only **relevant** education, experience, and projects.
- **ATS-Friendly formatting:**
  - Simple layout with standard section headings: *Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education*
  - Include keywords from the job description naturally
  - ❌ Avoid graphics, icons, text boxes, and complex tables — ATS can't read them
- Bullet points should start with **action verbs** and include **results** where possible.
- Proofread carefully for grammar and consistency.
- **Confidence tip:** Never show desperation during the interview — speak confidently, preferably in English.

---

## 🛠️ 2. Projects and Technologies You Must Be Able to Defend

> 💡 If it's on your CV, they will ask about it. Be ready to go deep.

- Only list projects and technologies you **truly understand**.
- If you built projects with AI assistance, learn **everything** used in them:
  - Architecture and end-to-end pipeline
  - Each major file/module and why it exists
  - Libraries, APIs, and deployment flow
- Be ready to explain:
  - Design choices
  - Trade-offs made
  - Implementation details

---

## 💡 3. Technical Preparation

- Practice core **data structures and algorithms** regularly.
- Review common problem patterns: arrays, strings, trees, graphs, dynamic programming.
- Practice writing **clean, explainable solutions out loud** — interviewers want to see your thought process.

---

## 🎭 4. Behavioural Preparation — STAR Method

Use the **STAR** framework for every behavioural question:

| Step | What to cover |
|------|--------------|
| **S**ituation | Set the scene — what was the context? |
| **T**ask | What was your responsibility? |
| **A**ction | What specific steps did you take? |
| **R**esult | What was the outcome? (quantify if possible) |

### Common Behavioural Questions to Prepare

| Question | What They're Testing |
|----------|---------------------|
| "Tell me about yourself" | Communication, self-awareness |
| "Describe a challenge you faced" | Problem-solving, resilience |
| "Tell me about a time you worked in a team" | Collaboration |
| "What is your greatest weakness?" | Self-awareness, honesty |
| "Why do you want this role?" | Motivation, research |

> 🔹 Keep each answer **concise and outcome-focused** — 90 seconds max.  
> 🔹 Practice your introduction fresh for **every role and company** — mention their specific product/stack.

---

## 🏢 5. Company and Role Research

> 💡 Researched candidates stand out immediately. Most don't do this.

Before every interview:

- **Product:** understand what the company actually builds and sells.
- **Stack:** check their job postings, LinkedIn, and GitHub for technology clues.
- **Team size and culture:** small team = more responsibility; large team = more specialization.
- **Location and reach:** domestic only vs. international presence matters for salary and growth.
- **Match your experience:** identify exactly which of your projects or skills overlap with their needs.
- **Salary:** think through your range and floor in advance so you don't stammer when asked.

---

## 🎯 6. Mock Interviews

- Do **timed** mock interviews with peers or use online platforms.
- Record yourself if possible — watch your pacing, filler words, and confidence.
- After each mock: identify weak areas and fix them **before** the real interview.

---

## 📅 7. Interview Day

| Checklist | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| 👔 Dress formally | First impression is the last impression |
| 🎤 Test your setup | Internet, mic, camera — 10 minutes before the call |
| ⏰ Join early | 5–10 minutes before the scheduled time |
| 🤔 Clarify before coding | Ask questions before you start solving — shows analytical thinking |
| 💬 Think out loud | Communicate your reasoning, not just the answer |
| ❓ Ask good questions | About team structure, product, onboarding — shows genuine interest |
| 📝 Request feedback | Politely ask for feedback at the end to show growth mindset |

---

## 📬 8. After the Interview

- Send a **short thank-you message** within 24 hours.
- Write down every question they asked.
- Note what went well and what to improve — every interview is practice.

---

## ❌ 9. Never Show Desperation

> 💡 You're not desperate — you're genuinely interested and you have options.

- ❌ Don't say: *"I really want this job, I'll do anything."*
- ❌ Don't be informal or overly casual with the interviewer.
- ✅ Do: be confident, curious, and professional.
- ✅ Do: let your research and relevant experience speak for itself.

The overall tone to maintain: **you're evaluating them just as much as they're evaluating you.**

---

## ✅ Quick Checklist Before Any Interview

```
[ ] Researched the company's product, team, and stack
[ ] Reviewed the job description and matched your experience
[ ] Practiced "Tell me about yourself" for this specific role
[ ] Prepared 3 STAR stories (challenge, teamwork, failure)
[ ] Prepared 3 thoughtful questions to ask them
[ ] Reviewed your CV projects — ready to explain every line
[ ] Decided on your salary expectation and floor
[ ] Tested internet, mic, and camera (for remote)
[ ] Planned to join 5–10 minutes early
```
